3 accused in Begusarai stripping case surrender


BEGUSARAI: The three absconding accused, wanted in the stripping case of a woman and a man in Begusarai, surrendered before the police on Sunday. Begusarai SP Yogendra Kumar said the police had been mounting continuous pressure on them and also initiated the process of attaching their property. "As a result, Ramjatan Paswan, Ravindra Thakur and Dilip Poddar, wanted in the case, surrendered before the SDPO at Teghra police station on Sunday," he said. The SP said the accused not only engaged in the disgraceful act of stripping and thrashing the woman, but also recorded the video of the incident and later made it viral on social media on Friday. "All the three accused belong to the same village under Teghra PS. The woman in the video turned out to be a minor and so the police have booked the accused under the POCSO Act, IT Act and SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act. I have ordered Teghra SDPO to file a chargesheet soon in the case so that speedy trial under a fast track court could be conducted," he said. The other accused in the case, a music teacher with whom the woman was found in an objectionable state, had already been arrested and sent to jail on Saturday after the woman in her statement to the police accused him of raping her.
