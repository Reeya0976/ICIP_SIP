One Rajya Sabha, 6 Lok Sabha seats: Chirag’s condition for joining NDA


PATNA: Ahead of the NDA meeting scheduled for July 18 in New Delhi, Lok Janshakti Party (LJP) led by Chirag Paswan has presented a new condition to the BJP leadership. The party is demanding all six Lok Sabha seats contested and won by the undivided LJP in 2019, and a Rajya Sabha seat previously held by party founder Ram Vilas Paswan. Sources within the party revealed that this decision was made during a meeting with senior party officials in Patna on Sunday, before Chirag departed for Delhi.
The LJP aims to firmly assert its claim over these “traditional” seats and the RS seat. When asked about the LJP demand, state BJP president Samrat Choudhary said the top BJP leadership was discussing the issue in Delhi. LJP has two factions, one led by Chirag and the other, Rashtriya Lok Janshakti Party (RLJP), led by his uncle and Union minister Pashupati Paras. Sources suggest BJP is frustrated with their animosity and wants them to resolve their differences promptly. Reports said it was under this plan that Union minister Nityanand Rai visited Paras a day before yesterday but the move has now annoyed the RLJP president. “Nityanand Rai ke kahne se kya hoga? Nityanand Raiji BJP ke authentic member nahin na hain (who is Nityanand Rai to ask? He is not an authentic member of the BJP),” Paras told the media. His observation drew bitter reactions from Chirag camp, which described it as an insult to the BJP and the alliance dharma.“This is a highly irresponsible statement by a union minister like Parasji showing Nityanand Raiji in poor light. He is breaking the BJP norms while being a part of its alliance.,” LJP (RV)’s national spokesperson Ashok Bhatt said on Sunday.
